How Much Does Home Renovation Cost in Malaysia? (2025 Price Guide)

✍️ John Lim One Stop Renovation
📍 KL · Selangor · Seremban
⏱ 8 min read

One of the first questions every Malaysian homeowner asks before renovating is: "How much will this actually cost?" It's a fair question — and one that deserves a straight answer, not a vague "it depends."

This guide breaks down renovation costs in Malaysia by room, by service type, and by property size, based on real 2025 market pricing. We've also included the key factors that drive costs up or down, so you can plan your budget with confidence.

Note: All prices below are estimates based on standard materials and workmanship. Actual costs vary by scope, materials chosen, and contractor. Always request a detailed itemised quote before committing.

What Drives Renovation Costs Up?

  • Material grade — The single biggest variable. Plywood vs particleboard in carpentry can differ by 30–50% in cost.
  • Demolition scope — Hacking walls, removing old tiles, or structural changes add significant cost.
  • High-rise access — Material hoisting, weekend work restrictions, and management fees at condominiums add to project costs.
  • Design complexity — Custom shapes, curves, detailed mouldings, and specialty finishes all require more skilled labour and time.
  • Subcontractor reliance — Contractors who outsource trades (carpentry, tiling, electrical) add markup at every level. In-house teams are more efficient and cost-effective.

How to Get the Best Value for Your Renovation Budget

  1. Get at least 3 itemised quotes — Never accept a lump-sum quote. Insist on a detailed line-by-line breakdown so you can compare fairly.
  2. Prioritise structural work first — M&E, plumbing, and structural changes should be done before any finishing. Doing it in the wrong order wastes money.
  3. Invest in carpentry quality — Cheap cabinets that warp or delaminate within 3 years cost more in the long run. Opt for plywood with quality laminate finishes.
  4. Use a contractor with in-house trades — Every subcontractor in the chain adds markup. John Lim Renovation handles carpentry, tiling, painting, and M&E in-house, keeping costs leaner.
  5. Confirm warranty terms in writing — A 6-month workmanship warranty (like ours) protects you from defects at no additional cost.
